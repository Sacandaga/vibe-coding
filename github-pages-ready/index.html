The bottom line

The Houthis' one-month strategic pause is the most important data point in this analysis. It proves three things: they can be deterred, they make rational survival calculations, and they are not simply an Iranian puppet that activates on command. That pause is a diplomatic opening — but it's closing. Every day without engagement through the Omani channel increases the probability that the Houthis will resume Red Sea attacks, creating an unprecedented dual-chokepoint crisis that could push oil past $170/barrel and trigger a global recession.

The 60:1 cost asymmetry means the US cannot defend its way out of this problem. The only sustainable solution is a negotiated framework that keeps Bab el-Mandeb open — and the only party that can broker that framework is Oman. The window is 4-6 weeks. After that, we're in a different world.
